Evans Monene Isaboke

Executive Director, and Team Leader Management, Strategy & Organizational Development

Isaboke is result-driven Management, Strategy, and Organizational Development leader and expert with over 25 years of experience guided by Christian values, integrity, accountability, transparency, professionalism, teamwork, and hard work. He has made remarkable achievements as a leader undertaking consultancy & research assignments and implementing development and humanitarian assistance programmes in Africa.

He has successfully worked closely with international development organizations, national governments, County or local governments, UN agencies, international funding organizations, NGOs, Civil Society organizations, community-based organizations, communities, beneficiaries, local leaders, churches, and church related organizations in Africa. Some of the organization he has engaged and partnered with include CODELP East Africa Consulting Limited (current), CORAT Africa, World Bank/International Finance Corporation, USAID Kenya and USAID Regional Office, Swedish International Development Agency (SIDA) Regional Office, Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA), World Food Programme (WFP), United Nations Children Fund (UNICEF), Korean International Cooperation Agency (KOICA), Global Fund, Born Free Foundation-USA, Africa Biodiversity Network, Fairtrade East and Central Africa Region, St. Gabriel Hospital Malawi-Lilongwe, The Kenya Government Ministries and Departments: Ministry of Environment, Water and Natural Resources; Water Resource Management Authority; Ministry of Planning and National Development-National Bureau of Statistics, Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development, Globethics.net – Switzerland Geneva, Arkangelo Ali Association South Sudan, Bread for the World Germany (BfdW) Germany-Bonn, Christian Solidarity International-Luxemburg, Presbyterian Relief and Development Agency South Sudan- Juba, Mission 21, Switzerland-Geneva, KoGe Community of Cooperation Switzerland- Geneva, Institute for Sustainable Development Ethiopia - Addis Ababa, Swedish Society of Nature Conservation Sweden- Stockholm, Act Church of Sweden – Uppsala, World Council of Churches Switzerland-Geneva, Christian Brothers of West Africa Sierra Leone- Freetown, South Sudan Relief and Development Agency-Juba, PanAfricare Kenya, Children Sickle Cell Foundation - Kenya, Our Lady of Charity of Good Shepherd Kenya-Nairobi, Sisters of Mercy Congregation Kenya-Nairobi, Presbyterian Church of Rwanda-Kigali, FECCLAHA Great Lakes and Horn of Africa-Nairobi, Transparency International Kenya, Programme for Christian-Muslim Relations in Africa (PROCMURA), Presbyterian Church of East Africa, Kikuyu Hospital-Kiambu, Newlife Seventh-Day Adventist Church-Kenya Nairobi, Africa Inland Church Health Ministries Kenya-Nairobi, Christian Mission for Development South Sudan, Seed of Peace Africa-Kenya, COPTIC Hospital in Kenya, Women Empowerment Link Kenya, Resource Oriented Development Initiatives Kenya, World Concern Africa Regional Office and Food for the Hungry International/Kenya-Nairobi.

Some of the leadership and influential positions Isaboke has served include Director & Leader of Management, Strategy and Organizational Development at CODELP East Africa Consulting Ltd (current), Consultancy and Research Manager CORAT Africa; Country Operations Manager at World Concern Africa; Country Director, Regional Coordinator and Programme Manager at Food for the Hungry International/Kenya; Agricultural Extension Officer at the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock Development; Church Elder at Newlife SDA Church Nairobi; Organizing Secretary Newlife SDA Church SACCO; and an Independent Consultant for over ten (10) years. During public service, he has built competence in core areas including research, project monitoring, project evaluations, strategy development and reviews, organizational development and assessments, project design and proposal development, policy development, financial management services, data collection and analysis, capacity development, governance and leadership development, business development, budgeting, administrative responsibilities; supervision, couching and mentorship of staff and teams and writing reports.

Isaboke is a European Union/Commission trained Project Cycle Management (PCM) expert that has consulted and managed many donors funded programmes and projects with required competency and dedication. He has led project management processes (planning, implementation, project design and proposal development, donor contract management, implementation of work plans, as well as staff capacity development) successfully working closely with various teams. Isaboke has rich experience in various sectors including education, agriculture, livelihoods, institutional development, water and sanitation, health, natural resources management, disaster risk reduction, and humanitarian aid among others. He has managed to travel widely in remote areas of Africa providing consultancy, research and training services in many countries including Uganda, Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Zambia, Rwanda, Burundi, Ghana, Liberia, Sierra Leone, Malawi, Mozambique, Zimbabwe, Benin, Nigeria, Cameroon and DR Congo among others.

Isaboke is professionally qualified with a Master of Arts degree in Project Planning and Management from the University of Nairobi, pursuing PhD in Project Planning and Management at the same university; Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics & Sociology; Postgraduate Diploma in Marketing Management; and Diploma in Agriculture. Other qualifications include training in Environmental Journalism and Communication, Advanced Agro-Technologies, Financial Sustainability, Donor Contract Management and Compliance, Ethical Leadership for prosperous nation, Ethical investment, Project Cycle Management, Financial and Economic Analysis of Projects, Communication and Report Writing, Management of Sustainability, Environmental Assessment & Management, Environmental Review, Project Design and Proposal Development among others.

Isaboke has passion for Africa and desires to engage organizations and people of Africa to help them find long-lasting solutions to the challenges and issues affecting them. He brings relevant skills and experiences to make a contribution to finding long-lasting solutions, outcomes and change in the lives of our people.
